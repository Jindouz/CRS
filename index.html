The Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is a majestic big cat found in Africa and small parts of India.
Known for their social structure, lions form prides led by males with impressive manes.
Facing conservation challenges, they are iconic symbols, representing strength and grace.
Conservation efforts focus on preserving their crucial ecological role and biodiversity.

The Tiger


The tiger (Panthera tigris) is a powerful big cat native to various parts of Asia.
Known for its distinctive orange coat with black stripes, tigers are solitary hunters, preying on a variety of animals.
Facing threats such as habitat loss and poaching, conservation efforts strive to protect these iconic and endangered predators.

The Cheetah

The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is a swift big cat found in parts of Africa and the Middle East.
Recognized for its slender build and distinctive black tear stripes, cheetahs are the fastest land animals.
Facing conservation challenges, they are known for their incredible speed and are adapted for high-speed chases when hunting prey.

The Leopard


The leopard (Panthera pardus) is a large wild cat known for its distinctive golden-yellow coat covered with rosette patterns.
Found across Africa and parts of Asia, leopards are adaptable predators with a broad diet.
They face conservation concerns, primarily habitat loss and illegal wildlife trade, threatening their survival.
